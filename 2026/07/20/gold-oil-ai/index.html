Gold, Oil, and the AI Bubble

Posted on 2026-07-20 Edited on 2026-07-21 Word count in article: 3.2k Reading time ≈ 12 mins.

I recently watched a thought-provoking conversation between Professor Di Dongsheng and Wang Xiao. Professor Di raised a fascinating thesis about the future of the AI investment boom.

His argument goes like this:

  • The AI bubble (if it is one) has been fuelled by massive, sustained capital inflows.
  • A significant portion of that “dry powder” comes from Middle Eastern PE funds – sovereign wealth vehicles that have benefited from decades of oil surpluses.
  • However, the ongoing geopolitical turmoil in the Middle East has severely disrupted their primary cash flow: petrodollar revenues.

The Gold–Oil Decoupling

Here’s where it gets interesting. Recently, we have observed a clear divergence between gold and crude oil prices:

  • Middle East tensions rise → oil prices spike (supply fears).
  • Yet, instead of safe‑haven gold rallying alongside oil, gold prices have dropped.

Professor Di interprets this as a liquidity squeeze: cash‑strapped Middle Eastern funds are selling their accumulated gold reserves to cover immediate fiscal needs. Gold becomes a source of liquidity, not a hedge, in times of their own distress.

The Inevitable Corollary

If this interpretation is correct, then we can derive a simple but powerful signal:

When the normal relationship resumes – i.e., oil spikes and gold spikes together during Middle East escalations – it will mean that gold reserves have been largely depleted.

At that point, these same PE funds may be forced to liquidate their most liquid risky assets to raise cash. And what is more liquid and over‑valued right now than AI startup stakes?

That could be the pin that pricks the AI bubble.
